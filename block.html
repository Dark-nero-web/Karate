Shotokan Karate Blocking Techniques (BLOCK)

👊 Uchi Uke (Inside Block)

BEGINNER

The fundamental inside block, used to defend against middle-level attacks. The forearm rotates inward to deflect incoming strikes. One of the most essential and frequently used defensive techniques in all of karate.

👊 Soto Uke (Outside Block)

BEGINNER

A powerful outward block that deflects attacks coming from the outside. The forearm rotates outward with the arm moving across the body. Essential for defending against kicks and strikes aimed at the torso.

👊 Age Uke (Rising Block)

BEGINNER

An upward blocking motion used to defend against overhead strikes and attacks to the head. The arm rises diagonally across the body with the forearm extended. Primary defense against high-level attacks.

👊 Gedan Barai (Low Block)

BEGINNER

A sweeping downward block used to defend against low kicks and attacks below the waist. The arm sweeps downward and outward from the body. Critical defensive technique against leg attacks and kicks.

👊 Shuto Uke (Knife Hand Block)

INTERMEDIATE

A block executed with the edge of the hand (knife hand) rather than the forearm. Powerful and precise, used for high-level blocks and counter-attacks. Requires proper hand conditioning.

👊 Kakiwake Uke (X-Block)

INTERMEDIATE

A simultaneous double block used to defend against strong attacks or to block two threats at once. Both arms cross to form an X shape. Provides maximum coverage against powerful strikes.

👊 Morote Uke (Double Block)

INTERMEDIATE

A reinforced block where both forearms work together to block a single attack, providing extra strength and stability. Used when defending against particularly powerful strikes.

👊 Nagashi Uke (Sweeping Block)

INTERMEDIATE

A flowing, circular block that sweeps incoming attacks off-line rather than meeting them head-on. Creates openings for counter-attacks. Advanced from simple linear blocks.

👊 Juji Uke (Cross Block)

INTERMEDIATE

Similar to Kakiwake Uke, this cross block can be performed high or low for different defensive situations. The arms cross to create a shield effect, defending against multi-directional attacks.

👊 Haiwan Uke (Back Arm Block)

ADVANCED

A block executed with the back of the forearm or arm, used for lateral defenses. Less common but effective when the standard forearm block cannot be applied. Requires good body positioning.

👊 Kossa Uke (Hip Block)

ADVANCED

A block where the hip and thigh are used to deflect and block low attacks. Provides stability and uses body mechanics. Advanced defensive technique combining footwork and hip rotation.

👊 Osae Uke (Pressing Block)

ADVANCED

A pressing downward block that not only blocks but also controls an opponent's arm or weapon. Used to take initiative and set up counter-attacks. Demonstrates advanced control and timing.
